FAQs

1. Why do you become a Designer?

I believe that being able to live a life of service to others is an enormous privilege. I focus on enhancing other people, share knowledge and build on each other – which always in a case of a friendships relationships. I only use as many resources as necessary to keep the design simple and accessible.

2. What is your design philosophy?

I have no philosophy and not interested in awards or design trends, but form follows function. I listen, I understand, and then I design with other people. The idea comes first, then prototype, and then further improvement based on what I have learned with other people — as simple as that.

3. Who have you helped in the past?

I believe that no one is too small to make a difference. I assist people ranging from solopreneurs to in-house product teams, from start-ups to tech companies. If you had an open-minded and can identify a problem, I do my best to find you a design solution (with you).

4. What motivates you at work?

I don’t need motivation. Motivation is emotional and not reliable. As a professional designer, I don’t have many privileges to wait for it. Discipline and a daily habit that doesn’t rely on motivation work much better for me. Discipline only forces me to start, and the continuing will be so much easier.

5. Have you done a project like mine before?

Chances are if I recently have done a similar project in the past few months — I probably won’t take your project as a courtesy to my client and professional advancements. Due to the rapid change in digital product, the past’s innovative solutions may not be relevant in today’s context. I often find myself exploring unrealistic yet intriguing ideas rather than repeat the same process over and over.

6. Where do you draw inspiration from?

To be specific, I’m not sure. I often find myself excited with a real-world problem from other people, the freeform shape of nature and its harmony, and the magical communication of music.

7. How do you acquire new knowledge?

I’m imagining, learning, and thinking by doing.

8. Any portfolio publication terms?

Just like other creatives and designer, I display the deliverables as a recognition of creative excellence and professional advancement (portfolio).In general, after I deliver the project, I will delay my portfolio up to 90 days — or as soon as the project goes public. I DO NOT work as a white-label.
